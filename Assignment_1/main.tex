Question :
We are given a triangle and the midpoints D, E, F of the sides AB, BC, AC respectively. We have to find the normal form of equation of lines AD, BE, CF.\\
Solution :
\begin{align}
\vec{A} = \myvec{1\\-1}\\
\vec{B} = \myvec{-4\\6}\\
\vec{C} = \myvec{-3\\-5}
\end{align}
The mid points D, E, F of sides AB, BC, AC are :-
\begin{align}
\vec{D} &= \myvec{\frac{-7}{2}\\\frac{1}{2}}\\
\vec{E} &= \myvec{-1\\-3}\\
\vec{F} &= \myvec{\frac{-3}{2}\\\frac{5}{2}}
\end{align}

Now, the direction vector of line, $\vec{m}$ is :
\begin{align}
\vec{m} &= \vec{D} - \vec{A}\\
	&= \myvec{\frac{-9}{2}\\\frac{3}{2}}
\end{align}

$\vec{n}$ can be written in the form :-
\begin{align}
\vec{n} &= \myvec{0&1\\-1&0}\vec{m}\\
	&= \myvec{0&1\\-1&0}\myvec{\frac{-9}{2}\\\frac{3}{2}}\\
	&= \myvec{\frac{3}{2}\\\frac{9}{2}}
\end{align}

The equation of line AD is :
\begin{align}
\vec{n}^{\top}(\vec{x} - \vec{A}) &= 0\\
\implies\vec{n}^{\top}\vec{x} &= \vec{n}^{\top}\vec{A}\\
\implies\myvec{\frac{3}{2}&\frac{9}{2}}\vec{x} &= \myvec{\frac{3}{2}&\frac{9}{2}}\myvec{1\\-1}\\
\implies\frac{3}{2}\myvec{1&3}\vec{x} &= \frac{3}{2}\myvec{1&3}\myvec{1\\-1}\\
\implies\myvec{1&3}\vec{x} &= \myvec{1&3}\myvec{1\\-1}\\
\implies\myvec{3&9}\vec{x} &= -2\\
\end{align}
